# Why is my credit card verification taking longer than expected for my account?
{: #ts-cc-verification}
{: troubleshoot}

You added a credit card to your Pay-As-You-Go or Enterprise Savings Plan account that was created through an {{site.data.keyword.IBM_notm}} seller, but the verification process is taking longer than expected.
{: shortdesc}

You added your credit card information to your account, but after several hours, your credit card is still not verified and you cannot use it for payments.
{: tsSymptoms}

For accounts created through an {{site.data.keyword.IBM_notm}} seller, the credit card verification process can take up to 30 minutes to complete. This verification period is required to validate your account information and ensure security and compliance, especially when switching from purchase order or firm order letter to credit card payment.
{: tsCauses}

Wait for the verification process to complete. You might need to log out and log back in to see the changes. 
{: note}

You can check the status of your credit card verification by completing the following steps:
{: tsResolve}

1. Go to the [Support Center](/unifiedsupport/supportcenter){: external} in the {{site.data.keyword.cloud_notm}} console.
2. Check for any open support cases related to your payment method change.
3. Review any email notifications that were sent to the billing contact for your account.

If the verification process takes longer than 30 minutes, or if you receive an error notification, contact {{site.data.keyword.cloud_notm}} Support by creating a case from the Support Center.

During the verification period, your account remains active, but you cannot make payments using the credit card until verification is complete. If you have an urgent payment due, consider using a one-time payment option or contact {{site.data.keyword.cloud_notm}} Support for assistance.
